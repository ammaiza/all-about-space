History

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and by far the largest. Jupiter is more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined :

Jupiter is the fourth brightest object in the sky. It has been known since prehistoric times as a bright "wandering star". But in 1610 when Galileo first pointed a telescope at the sky he discovered Jupiter's four large moons known as the Galilean moons recorded their motions back and forth around Jupiter..

Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system. It's similar to a star, but it never got big enough to start burning. It is covered in swirling cloud stripes. It has big storms like the Great Red Spot, which has been going for hundreds of years. Jupiter is a gas giant and doesn't have a solid surface, but it may have a solid inner core about the size of Earth. Jupiter also has rings, but they're too faint to see very well.

Eleven Earths could fit across Jupiter’s equator. If Earth were the size of a grape, Jupiter would be the size of a basketball.

Mercury makes a complete orbit around the sun (a year in Mercury time) in just 88 Earth days.

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a gigantic storm that’s about twice the size of Earth and has raged for over a century.

Mercury's thin atmosphere, or exosphere, is composed mostly of oxygen, sodium , hydrogen , helium , and potassium

It's ringless and moonless.

No evidence for life has been found on Mercury. Daytime Temperatures can reach 430 degrees Celsius and drop to -180 degrees Celsius at night. It is unlikely life (as we know it) could survive on this planet.

Fun Fact: Standing on Jupiter's surface at its closest point to the sun, the sun would appear more than three times larger than it does on Earth.!
